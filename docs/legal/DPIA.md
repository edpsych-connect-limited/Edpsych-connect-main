# DATA PROTECTION IMPACT ASSESSMENT (DPIA)

**Project:** EdPsych Connect World Platform
**Date:** December 5, 2025

## 1. Description of Processing
The platform processes sensitive personal data (special category data regarding health and education needs) of children and young people to support SEND provision and EHCP management.

## 2. Assessment of Necessity and Proportionality
Processing is necessary to fulfill statutory obligations under the Children and Families Act 2014 and to provide effective educational psychology support. Data minimization principles are applied.

## 3. Risks to Rights and Freedoms
- **Risk:** Unauthorized access to sensitive student data.
  - **Mitigation:** AES-256 encryption, strict RBAC, MFA, audit logging.
- **Risk:** Data loss or corruption.
  - **Mitigation:** Automated backups, geographic redundancy.
- **Risk:** Inappropriate data sharing.
  - **Mitigation:** System-enforced permission boundaries, consent management.

## 4. Consultation
Consultation has been undertaken with educational psychologists, schools, and data privacy experts.

## 5. Measures to Protect Data
- Encryption at rest and in transit.
- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC).
- Regular penetration testing.
- Staff training on data protection.

## 6. Conclusion
The residual risk after mitigation is considered low and acceptable given the significant benefit to the data subjects (improved educational support).
