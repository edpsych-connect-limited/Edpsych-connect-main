## Your First Assessment

### Step 1: Creating an Assessment

1. Click **Assessments** from your dashboard
2. Click **+ New Assessment**
3. Select **ECCA Framework** (recommended)
4. Enter student details:
   - Name
   - Date of birth / Age
   - School and year group
   - Reason for referral

### Step 2: Reviewing the Framework

The ECCA Framework covers four cognitive domains:

1. **Working Memory & Information Processing**
   - Verbal and visual memory
   - Processing capacity
   - Learning potential

2. **Attention & Executive Function**
   - Sustained attention
   - Selective attention
   - Executive control

3. **Processing Speed**
   - Cognitive efficiency
   - Response speed
   - Automaticity

4. **Learning & Memory**
   - Encoding strategies
   - Retrieval processes
   - Learning patterns

### Step 3: Observation Guidelines

For each domain, you'll:

1. **Read the indicators** - Behavioral descriptions to look for
2. **Observe the child** - In structured and unstructured settings
3. **Rate performance** - On provided scales
4. **Apply Test-Teach-Retest** - Assess learning potential:
   - Initial performance
   - Teach a strategy
   - Observe improvement
5. **Document observations** - Rich, descriptive notes

**Example Observation:**
> "During the digit span task, Jake correctly recalled 4 digits forward but struggled with 3 backward. When taught the chunking strategy (grouping numbers), his backward span improved to 4-5 digits, demonstrating good learning potential and responsiveness to intervention."

### Step 4: Inviting Collaborators

The ECCA Framework uses multi-informant input. You can invite:

**Parents/Caregivers:**
- Home observations
- Developmental history
- Family context

**Teachers:**
- Classroom performance
- Peer comparisons
- Learning patterns

**The Child:**
- Self-perception (age-appropriate)
- Preferences
- Aspirations

**To invite a collaborator:**
1. Go to the **Collaborative Input** step
2. Click **+ Invite Contributor**
3. Select contributor type
4. Enter name and email
5. Add a personal message (optional)
6. Click **Send Invitation**

The contributor receives a secure, one-time link to complete their input without needing a platform account.

### Step 5: Synthesizing Information

After gathering all input:

1. Review responses from all informants
2. Identify patterns and discrepancies
3. Consider context (home vs. school)
4. Formulate professional interpretation
5. Generate evidence-based recommendations

## Understanding ECCA Framework

### Evidence Base

ECCA is grounded in three major theoretical frameworks:

**1. Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development**
- Assesses not just current performance, but learning potential
- Identifies what the child can do with support
- Informs scaffolding strategies

**2. Feuerstein's Mediated Learning Experience**
- Test-Teach-Retest methodology
- Assesses responsiveness to intervention
- Distinguishes between performance and competence

**3. Diamond's Executive Function Model**
- Comprehensive cognitive profiling
- Domain-specific assessment
- Developmental appropriateness

### Why Dynamic Assessment?

Traditional standardized tests provide:
- Snapshot of current performance
- Norm-referenced scores
- Static picture

ECCA adds:
- Learning potential assessment
- Response to intervention
- Dynamic, contextualized understanding
- Strengths-based profiling

### Interpreting Results

**High Responsiveness to Teaching:**
- Good learning potential
- Benefits from explicit instruction
- Likely to make progress with appropriate support

**Moderate Responsiveness:**
- Requires more intensive support
- May need modified approaches
- Consider alternative strategies

**Limited Responsiveness:**
- May indicate more significant difficulties
- Requires specialist assessment
- Consider statutory assessment

### Why Multi-Informant?

Research shows:
- Different informants observe different contexts
- Home and school performance can vary significantly
- Child's self-perception is valuable
- Comprehensive picture improves intervention planning

## Finding Evidence-Based Interventions

### The Interventions Library

- Literacy
- Numeracy
- Social-Emotional Learning
- Behavior Management
- Executive Function
- Attention & Focus

### Understanding Evidence Ratings

**Strong Evidence (Effect Size > 0.4):**
- Rigorous research base
- Consistently positive outcomes
- Recommended as first-line

**Moderate Evidence (Effect Size 0.2-0.4):**
- Some research support
- Generally positive outcomes
- Worth trying with monitoring

**Emerging Evidence (Effect Size < 0.2 or limited studies):**
- Limited research
- Promising practice
- Requires careful monitoring

### Selecting an Intervention

**Matching to Needs:**
1. Identify specific difficulty from assessment
2. Filter interventions by category
3. Check age appropriateness
4. Review evidence rating
5. Consider resources needed

**Example Decision Process:**

**Student Profile:**
- Age: 8 years
- Need: Phonological awareness difficulties
- Context: Mainstream school
- Resources: TA support available

**Filter:**
- Category: Literacy
- Age: 5-11 years
- Evidence: Strong

**Result:**
- **Phonics Intervention Programme**
- Effect size: +0.54
- Duration: 12 weeks, 20 min daily
- Materials: £150
- Training: 2 hours

## Creating and Managing EHCPs

### Starting a New EHCP

1. Navigate to **EHCP** section
2. Click **+ New EHCP**
3. Enter child details
4. Complete sections in order

### Section-by-Section Guide

**Section A: The Child or Young Person's Views**

**Purpose:** Capture the child's voice

**Best Practice:**
- Use direct quotes where possible
- Age-appropriate methods (drawings, conversations)
- Focus on preferences, aspirations, concerns
- Include what the child enjoys

**Example:**
> "I like PE and art. Reading is hard for me. I want to be a footballer when I grow up."

---

**Section B: Special Educational Needs**

**Purpose:** Describe needs across four areas

**Areas:**
1. Cognition and Learning
2. Communication and Interaction
3. Social, Emotional, and Mental Health
4. Sensory and/or Physical

**Structure:**
- Describe specific difficulties
- Link to assessment evidence
- Use clear, specific language
- Avoid jargon

**Example:**
> **Cognition and Learning:** Assessment using the ECCA Framework indicates significant working memory difficulties. Jake demonstrates particular challenges with verbal information processing, recalling only 4 digits forward compared to age expectation of 6-7. This impacts his ability to follow multi-step instructions and complete tasks independently in the classroom.

---

**Section C-D: Health and Social Care Needs**
- Input from health professionals
- Social care involvement
- Coordinate with other services

---

**Section E: Outcomes**

**SMART Outcomes:**
- **S**pecific
- **M**easurable
- **A**chievable
- **R**elevant
- **T**ime-bound

**Example:**
✅ Good: "Jake will increase his reading age by 6 months over the academic year, as measured by NGRT in July 2026."

❌ Vague: "Jake will improve his reading."

---

**Section F: Provision**

**Must specify:**
- **What:** Specific intervention/support
- **Who:** Qualified practitioner
- **Where:** Setting/context
- **How often:** Frequency and duration
- **How monitored:** Review arrangements

**Example:**
> 20 minutes daily 1:1 phonics intervention (Read Write Inc.) delivered by trained Teaching Assistant, reviewed termly via phonics screening and reading assessments.

---

**Section G-H: Placement and Personal Budget**
- Appropriate setting
- Budget if applicable

## Tips and Best Practices

### Assessment Tips

**1. Gather Multiple Data Sources**
- Classroom observations
- Work samples
- Standardized tests
- Multi-informant input

**2. Consider Context**
- Home vs. school performance
- Cultural factors
- Recent changes (bereavement, moving house)
- Medical factors

**3. Focus on Learning Potential**
- What can the child do with support?
- How quickly do they learn new strategies?
- What scaffolding is most effective?

**4. Be Strengths-Based**
- Identify what the child CAN do
- Build interventions on strengths
- Maintain child's motivation

### Intervention Tips

**1. Start with Evidence**
- Choose interventions with strong research base
- Understand why it works
- Check age appropriateness

**2. Implement with Fidelity**
- Follow the program as designed
- Don't skip steps
- Use provided materials
- Maintain consistency

**3. Monitor Progress Regularly**
- Weekly or fortnightly measures
- Use visual graphs
- Adjust if no progress after 4-6 weeks

**4. Engage Parents**
- Share progress
- Teach strategies for home
- Celebrate small wins

### EHCP Tips

**1. Link Needs to Provision**
- Every need should have corresponding provision
- Provision should address the need

**2. Make Outcomes SMART**
- Use measurable criteria
- Specify assessment methods
- Set realistic timescales

**3. Be Specific About Provision**
- Avoid vague language
- Quantify (20 minutes daily)
- Specify who, what, where, how

**4. Annual Reviews**
- Start planning 2 months before
- Gather updated assessments
- Involve all stakeholders
- Document changes clearly

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**"I can't see my assessment"**
- Check you're logged into the correct account
- Use the search function
- Check filters (status, date range)

**"Collaborator says link doesn't work"**
- Check invitation was sent (view status)
- Ask them to check spam folder
- Resend invitation
- Links expire after 30 days

**"Report won't generate"**
- Ensure all required fields completed
- Check all domains have observations
- Try refreshing the page
- Contact support if persists

**"I made a mistake in an EHCP"**
- Click Edit on the relevant section
- Update information
- Save changes
- Version history is maintained

**"Lost password"**
- Click "Forgot Password" on login
- Enter your email address
- Check inbox for reset link
- Create new password
