COURSE: Dyslexia Intervention Strategies
MODULE: 3: Phonological Awareness: Foundation for Reading
LESSON: 1: What is Phonological Awareness? Continuum of Skills
INSTRUCTOR: Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick (Male Avatar)
---------------------------------------------------

PHONOLOGICAL AWARENESS: Ability to hear, identify, and manipulate sounds in spoken language (NOT written - purely oral). Critical predictor of reading success. Dyslexic students have significant deficits here. CONTINUUM (simple → complex): 1) Word awareness (sentences have words), 2) Syllable awareness (butterfly = but-ter-fly), 3) Onset-rime (cat = /c/ + /at/), 4) PHONEMIC AWARENESS (individual phonemes: cat = /c/ /a/ /t/) - most critical and most difficult. PHONEMIC AWARENESS SKILLS: Isolation (first sound in "cat"? /c/), Blending (/c/ /a/ /t/ = cat), Segmentation (cat = /c/ /a/ /t/), Deletion (cat without /c/ = at), Substitution (change /c/ to /b/ = bat). Must be taught explicitly to dyslexic students - doesn't develop naturally.
