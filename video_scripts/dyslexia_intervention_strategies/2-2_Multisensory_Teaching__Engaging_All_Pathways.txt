LESSON: 2: Multisensory Teaching: Engaging All Pathways
INSTRUCTOR: Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick (Male Avatar)
---------------------------------------------------

MULTISENSORY: Teaching that engages visual, auditory, kinesthetic, and tactile pathways simultaneously - strengthens memory and learning for dyslexic students. VISUAL: Letter cards, color coding, visual cues. AUDITORY: Say sounds aloud, listen for sounds in words, oral blending/segmenting. KINESTHETIC: Skywriting (large arm movements), tracing letters in air, movement while learning. TACTILE: Sand trays, textured letters, finger-tracing. EXAMPLE: Teaching /m/ sound - SEE the letter m (visual), HEAR the /m/ sound (auditory), SAY /m/ while writing (auditory-motor), TRACE m in sand (tactile-kinesthetic). Research shows multisensory instruction significantly more effective for dyslexia than single-pathway teaching.
