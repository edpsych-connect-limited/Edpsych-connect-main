## Script

[CONCERN] It's Thursday afternoon. The phone rings. It's the local authority—there's been a complaint from a parent. Their child's EHCP provision hasn't been delivered properly. There might be a tribunal coming.

And you think: I had no idea there was a problem.

[PAUSE]

[THOUGHTFUL] How many times have crises arrived like that? Not preceded by gradual warning signs you recognised, but sudden, seemingly out of nowhere, consuming days or weeks to resolve?

[SLOW] The truth is, most EHCP problems don't come from nowhere. They build. There are patterns. Early indicators. Small things that, if noticed, could have been addressed before they became big things.

[ENERGY] The Compliance and Risk module uses artificial intelligence to spot those patterns. Not to replace your professional judgement—but to ensure nothing slips through when you're overwhelmed with fifty other priorities.

**What Does "AI-Powered" Actually Mean?**

[WARMTH] Let me demystify this, because "AI" sounds either magical or terrifying depending on who you ask.

[THOUGHTFUL] The system analyses patterns across your EHCP caseload. It looks at things like:
- How long since the last annual review for each child?
- Are provision hours being recorded as delivered?
- Have outcome progress ratings been updated?
- Are there communication patterns suggesting parental concern?
- How does this child's trajectory compare to similar children?

[PAUSE]

[ENERGY] And then it calculates a risk score. Not a judgement—a probability. "Based on the patterns we're seeing, this case has a higher-than-usual probability of dispute or non-compliance."

[WARMTH] Think of it like weather forecasting. We can't tell you it will definitely storm. But we can tell you there's a 70% chance of rain—so maybe bring an umbrella.

**The Risk Dashboard**

[SERIOUS] When you open the Compliance and Risk module, you see your caseload ranked by risk score.

Red zone: High risk. Cases where multiple indicators are flashing. Overdue reviews. Incomplete provision records. Parent communication flagged as escalating. These need immediate attention.

Amber zone: Elevated risk. Something's not quite right. Maybe provision delivery is inconsistent. Maybe outcomes haven't been reviewed in a while. Worth investigating.

Green zone: On track. Reviews happening on time. Provision recorded. Progress documented. These cases are healthy.

[THOUGHTFUL] Now, here's the critical point. The system doesn't tell you what's wrong. It tells you where to look. You bring the professional judgement about what's actually happening and what to do about it.

**The Seven Risk Factors**

[ENERGY] Through analysis of thousands of EHCP cases, we've identified seven factors most predictive of problems:

**1. Annual Review Delays** - Reviews more than two months overdue significantly increase dispute risk.

**2. Provision Recording Gaps** - When provision hours aren't being logged, it's often because they're not being delivered—or not being documented. Either is problematic.

**3. Outcome Stagnation** - Outcomes rated the same for two or more years without re-evaluation suggest the plan isn't working.

**4. Communication Volume Spikes** - A sudden increase in parent emails or calls often precedes formal complaints.

**5. Professional Report Delays** - When therapist or specialist reports stop coming, provision might have stopped too.

**6. Phase Transfer Proximity** - Children approaching phase transfers without enhanced planning are at elevated risk.

**7. Historical Dispute Pattern** - Cases with prior mediations or tribunal involvement need closer monitoring.

[PAUSE]

[WARMTH] The module weights these factors differently based on your school's specific patterns. Over time, it learns what predicts problems in your context.

**Case Study: The Early Warning That Saved a Relationship**

[THOUGHTFUL] Let me share a real example—anonymised, of course.

A Year 4 child with an EHCP for autism. Annual reviews happening on time. Provision recorded. Everything looked fine.

But the system flagged elevated risk. Why?

[PAUSE]

Communication volume. The parent had sent 23 emails in three weeks—up from an average of 2 per month. And sentiment analysis showed language shifting: questions becoming demands, "I wonder if" becoming "I expect that."

[WARMTH] The SENCO called the parent. Not about any specific issue—just to check in. "I noticed we've been exchanging a lot of emails lately. Is everything okay?"

It turned out the parent had noticed their child becoming increasingly anxious. They weren't sure why. They weren't yet ready to escalate—but they were worried, and that worry was coming out in correspondence.

[ENERGY] One phone call. One conversation. The SENCO arranged an additional review. They identified the anxiety triggers—changes in classroom support staff that nobody had flagged. Adjustments were made. Anxiety reduced.

[SLOW] No complaint. No mediation. No tribunal. Because the system noticed what humans might have missed, and the human did what systems never could—built relationship.

**Compliance Reporting**

[SERIOUS] Beyond individual case risk, the module tracks your overall compliance position.

- Percentage of annual reviews completed on time
- Average time from EHCP request to finalisation
- Provision delivery rates across your caseload
- Outcome achievement patterns

[WARMTH] This isn't about judgement. It's about visibility. When Ofsted asks about your SEND provision, when your local authority conducts their annual review, when governors want to understand SEND effectiveness—you have the data.

**Alerts and Actions**

[ENERGY] The system doesn't just identify risk; it suggests actions.

A case enters the red zone? The module prompts: "Consider scheduling urgent parent conversation." "Check provision delivery records for gaps." "Review recent professional reports for concerns."

[THOUGHTFUL] You can accept these suggestions, dismiss them, or add notes explaining why the risk score isn't concerning in this particular case. Everything documented. Everything transparent.

**The Ethical Question**

[SLOW] Now, I need to address something important. Using AI to assess risk in children's education feels uncomfortable to some people. And that discomfort is valid.

[PAUSE]

[WARMTH] Here's how we've thought about it. The AI doesn't make decisions. It doesn't label children. It doesn't replace professional judgement. It surfaces patterns that might otherwise go unnoticed in an overwhelming caseload.

[SERIOUS] The alternative isn't no-AI; it's less information. It's crises that weren't predicted. It's children who slipped through gaps because humans were doing their best but couldn't see everything at once.

[ENERGY] We built the system to augment your expertise, not replace it. You remain the professional. The AI is just a very attentive assistant.

**Closing**

[THOUGHTFUL] The best time to prevent a crisis is before anyone knows a crisis is coming. That's what the Compliance and Risk module offers—early warning. Time to act before things escalate.

[PAUSE]

[WARMTH] I've seen SENCOs transform their practice with this tool. Not because they weren't already good at their jobs—they were—but because they finally had visibility they'd never had before.

[ENERGY] Fewer surprises. More proactive conversations. Relationships repaired before they broke. Children supported before they struggled.

[SLOW] That's what AI in education should be. Not surveillance. Not automation. Just intelligent support for the intelligent work you're already doing.
