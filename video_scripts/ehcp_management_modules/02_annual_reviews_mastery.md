## Script

[CONCERN] February half-term. You're supposed to be catching up on everything. But there you are, at 11pm, realising you've completely forgotten about Year 6 Sarah's annual review—the one that needed to happen before Christmas because she's transitioning to secondary.

[PAUSE]

Sound familiar?

[THOUGHTFUL] The thing about annual reviews is they're not actually annual. They're this constantly shifting landscape of due dates, dependent on when each EHCP was finalised, when the child's birthday falls, whether there's a phase transfer coming, whether the local authority randomly decided to amend something...

[ENERGY] It's like trying to juggle while someone keeps throwing new balls at you. And each ball is a child who deserves your full attention.

[WARMTH] Let me show you how the Annual Reviews module changes this.

[PAUSE]

[SLOW] Imagine your calendar isn't a static grid anymore. It's alive. It knows that Marcus's review is in April, but because he's Year 8 and his EHCP includes preparation for adulthood outcomes, it automatically flagged him for enhanced transition review in September—six months earlier than you'd normally start planning.

[ENERGY] That's not a reminder you set. That's the system understanding the SEND Code of Practice and applying it to your actual caseload.

**The Dashboard View**

When you log in, you see everything at once. Green dots—reviews completed on time. Amber—coming up in the next 6 weeks. Red—overdue or high priority. Click any one, and you're into that child's review space.

[WARMTH] But here's what I love most. That status isn't just about dates. The system knows if paperwork is missing. If the parent hasn't confirmed attendance. If the health professional's report still hasn't arrived. Amber might mean "review is next month" but it might also mean "review is next month and you're still waiting on three contributions."

**The Statutory Timeline Tracker**

[SERIOUS] Let's talk about the law, because the law matters here.

Annual reviews must happen within 12 months of the previous review. Phase transfer reviews must be completed by specific dates—15th February for secondary transfer, 31st March for post-16. For early years, there are additional rules around reviewing before starting school.

[PAUSE]

The module tracks all of this automatically. But more than that—it gives you early warnings. Not just "this is due next week" but "if you don't send invitations today, you won't meet the statutory notice period."

[THOUGHTFUL] I've seen SENCOs keep spreadsheets with these calculations. Three tabs, colour-coded formulas, copying dates manually. It works—until it doesn't. Until you're ill for a week, or a new child arrives mid-year, or the local authority changes their transfer timeline.

**Parent and Professional Invitations**

[WARMTH] Parents are the experts on their children. The Code of Practice is clear about this. But getting them engaged in the review process?

The module generates invitation letters automatically—but they're not cold, bureaucratic templates. We worked with parents to write them. They explain what the review is for, what the parent's contribution means, how their voice shapes the plan.

[ENERGY] One click sends invitations to everyone: parents, class teacher, teaching assistants, speech therapist, occupational therapist, the educational psychologist who last assessed, the social worker if there is one. Each person gets the right template, the right information, the right request.

And then it tracks responses. Accepted. Declined. No response yet. You know exactly who you're waiting on.

**The Review Meeting Itself**

[SLOW] The module doesn't run your meeting—that's your expertise, your relationships, your professional judgement. But it does give you everything you need going in.

A pre-populated agenda linked to last year's outcomes. Progress data from the integrated assessment system. Previous reports from all contributors. A draft recommendations section where you can capture live notes.

[WARMTH] And after the meeting? The system helps you generate the review paperwork. Not boilerplate—actual reflections on this child's progress toward their specific outcomes. Recommendations that flow logically from what was discussed.

**Outcome Tracking Over Time**

[THOUGHTFUL] Here's something that breaks my heart about traditional annual reviews. Each one exists in isolation. Paper in a folder. Digital file in a directory. You can't easily see: Is this child actually making progress toward their EHCP outcomes across years?

[ENERGY] This module changes that. Every review feeds into a longitudinal view. Outcome 1: Communication. Here's what we said in 2022. Here's what we said in 2023. Here's this year. A child's journey, visible at a glance.

[SERIOUS] This matters for tribunals too. If a case escalates, that coherent narrative of progress and provision is exactly what you need. Not "I think we talked about this..." but documented evidence of your professional practice.

**The Compliance Reality Check**

[CONCERN] Look, I'm not going to pretend annual reviews always go smoothly. Deadlines get missed. Local authorities don't respond on time. Parents don't engage. Health professionals cancel.

[WARMTH] The module can't fix all of that. But it can tell you exactly where you stand. Your compliance rate. Which reviews are at risk. Where the bottlenecks are—is it always the same professional not responding? Is it always Phase Transfer reviews that go over time?

[PAUSE]

That data lets you have different conversations. With your head teacher about workload. With the local authority about their responsiveness. With partner agencies about protocols.

**Closing**

[SLOW] Every child with an EHCP deserves an annual review that genuinely reflects on their progress and plans for their future. Not a box-ticking exercise. Not a rushed meeting because you ran out of time.

[ENERGY] The Annual Reviews module won't make you a better SENCO. You're already brilliant at what you do. But it will clear away enough of the administrative burden that you can actually do it.

[WARMTH] In the next video, we'll explore the Mediation and Tribunal module—because sometimes, despite everyone's best efforts, things escalate. Let me show you how to be ready.
