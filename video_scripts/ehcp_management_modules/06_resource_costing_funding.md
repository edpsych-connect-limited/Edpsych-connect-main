[ENERGY] The Resource Costing module makes this manageable. Let me walk you through it.

**The Three Elements: UK SEND Funding Explained**

[SERIOUS] In England, funding for children with EHCPs comes through three elements. If you don't know these, everything else will be confusing. So let's get clear.

**Element 1: Basic Per-Pupil Funding**
Every child in a mainstream school generates basic per-pupil funding through the National Funding Formula. This covers normal classroom teaching, standard resources, typical class sizes. Nothing special about this—it's the same for every child.

[PAUSE]

**Element 2: The Notional SEND Budget**
Schools receive a notional SEND budget—approximately £6,000 per pupil with additional needs—to provide support before an EHCP is in place and to meet basic EHCP provision. This comes from the main school budget. It's expected to cover the first £6,000 of additional support.

[THOUGHTFUL] "Notional" is the key word. It's not ring-fenced money that arrives separately. It's the expectation that schools will allocate this much from their overall budget toward SEND provision.

[ENERGY] **Element 3: Top-Up Funding**
This is the one everyone cares about. When an EHCP specifies provision that costs more than £6,000, the local authority should provide top-up funding to cover the excess. This is the "additional" money—above and beyond what schools are expected to provide themselves.

[PAUSE]

[SERIOUS] So if a child needs £18,000 of provision, the calculation is:
- Element 1: Basic per-pupil (included in normal budget)
- Element 2: First £6,000 from school's notional SEND budget
- Element 3: Remaining £12,000 requested as top-up from LA

[WARMTH] Simple in theory. Complicated in practice. Because who decides what provision costs? How do you calculate it? How do you prove it?

**Costing EHCP Provision**

[THOUGHTFUL] The Resource Costing module helps you calculate what provision actually costs.

Let's say an EHCP specifies:
- 15 hours per week 1:1 teaching assistant support
- 1 hour per week speech and language therapy
- 2 hours per week occupational therapy

[ENERGY] The module knows standard rates. TA hourly costs (including on-costs like National Insurance, pension). Therapy session costs. Resource costs. Training costs.

Enter the provision hours, and it calculates annual costs automatically. Not estimates—actual calculations based on your school's pay scales or standard LA rates.

[WARMTH] Suddenly, that conversation with the finance director changes:

"We need an additional teaching assistant."
"What's the cost?"
"£14,850 per year based on these hours at Band 3 rates plus on-costs."
"What does the LA provide?"
"Top-up covers £8,850. Our contribution is £6,000 from notional SEND budget."
"That's clear. Let's proceed."

**Band Funding Systems**

[THOUGHTFUL] Many local authorities use banding systems. Instead of costing every EHCP individually, they assign children to funding bands based on need complexity.

Band A might be £3,000 top-up. Band B might be £6,000. Band C might be £10,000. And so on.

[PAUSE]

[CONCERN] The problem? Banding can be crude. A child who needs £7,500 of provision gets placed in Band B at £6,000—leaving schools to find the gap. Or they get placed in Band C at £10,000—which might seem generous but locks resources into one child when they could be distributed more flexibly.

[ENERGY] The module tracks both. Actual provision costs AND band allocation. So you can see: Are we over-resourced or under-resourced for this child? Across the whole caseload, what's the gap between allocated funding and required provision?

**Cost-Effectiveness Conversations**

[WARMTH] Here's a sensitive topic that we need to discuss honestly.

Not all provision is equally cost-effective. Two different approaches might achieve the same outcomes—but one costs twice as much. Sometimes the expensive option is necessary. Sometimes it isn't.

[PAUSE]

[THOUGHTFUL] The module doesn't tell you which provision to choose. But it does show you the cost implications of different options. If you're considering 20 hours of TA support versus 10 hours plus a specific intervention programme, what's the cost difference? What does research suggest about effectiveness?

[SERIOUS] This isn't about cutting corners. It's about stewardship. Public money should be spent effectively. Every pound saved on one child's provision—without reducing outcomes—is a pound available for another child who needs support.

**Tracking Provision Delivery**

[ENERGY] Money allocated isn't the same as money spent. Provision specified isn't the same as provision delivered.

The module integrates with provision mapping. Hours specified in the EHCP. Hours actually delivered each week. Gaps identified automatically.

[CONCERN] Why does this matter for costing? Because if you're receiving top-up funding for 20 hours per week but only delivering 15 hours, there's a financial and ethical problem. The module flags this—not to punish you, but so you can address it.

**Budget Forecasting**

[THOUGHTFUL] SENCOs are increasingly asked to forecast SEND budgets. How much will we need next year? What about the year after?

The module helps with this. Based on current caseload, known phase transfers (children joining and leaving), and application pipeline, it projects future costs.

[WARMTH] Not precise predictions—education doesn't work that way. But informed estimates that support budget conversations.

"Based on current trends, we expect SEND top-up requirements to increase by approximately 12% next year due to three complex new EHCPs and one expensive Year 6 transfer."

That's a conversation you can have with senior leaders. That's planning, not just reacting.

**Audit Readiness**

[SERIOUS] Local authorities audit EHCP spending. They want to know: Is the money being spent as intended? Are outcomes being achieved? Is provision being delivered?

The module generates audit-ready reports. Per-child spending breakdowns. Provision delivery rates. Outcome progress linked to resource allocation.

[ENERGY] When the auditors arrive, you're not scrambling through spreadsheets. You're presenting organised evidence of responsible stewardship.

**Closing**

[WARMTH] Look, I know money isn't why you got into education. You're in this because you care about children. Because you believe every child deserves support to thrive.

[PAUSE]

[THOUGHTFUL] But money is how that support gets delivered. Teaching assistants need salaries. Therapists need paying. Resources need purchasing. Understanding the funding system isn't selling out—it's being effective.

[SLOW] The Resource Costing module won't magically increase your budget. It won't fix the structural underfunding of SEND in England—that's a political problem.

[ENERGY] But it will ensure you understand exactly what you have, exactly what you need, and exactly how to argue for the difference.

[WARMTH] Knowledge is power. And when you're fighting for resources for children who need them, you want all the power you can get.

[SLOW] Next up: The Golden Thread. The most important module of all—because this is where needs, provisions, and outcomes finally connect.
