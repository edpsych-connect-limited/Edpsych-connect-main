**What Are SEN2 Returns?**

[WARMTH] For those who might be new to this, let me explain.

Every January, local authorities must submit statistical returns to the Department for Education about special educational needs in their area. Schools provide data that feeds into this. The returns cover:

- Numbers of children with EHCPs
- Primary and secondary needs categories
- Placement types
- Age distributions
- New EHCPs issued during the year
- Ceased EHCPs
- Timelines met and missed

[SERIOUS] The data shapes national understanding of SEND provision. It influences policy. It affects funding formulae. It matters.

[PAUSE]

[THOUGHTFUL] But—and this is important—the data collection process was designed decades ago. Before modern databases. Before integrated systems. It assumes you're going to manually count, categorise, and report.

[ENERGY] We assume differently.

**Automated Data Extraction**

[WARMTH] Every child in your EHCP caseload has a record. Primary need. Secondary need. Date EHCP finalised. Date of last amendment. Placement type. Age. Phase.

The SEN2 Returns module extracts this data automatically. Not from separate spreadsheets you have to maintain—from the live system you're already using.

[PAUSE]

[ENERGY] Click "Generate SEN2 Data." The system pulls everything relevant. Categorises by DfE-required codes. Groups by the breakdowns the return requires. Presents it in the exact format the local authority needs.

What used to take days happens in seconds.

**Validation Before Submission**

[CONCERN] But extraction isn't enough. Raw numbers often contain errors. A child miscategorised. A date recorded wrong. A placement type that doesn't match DfE codes.

[THOUGHTFUL] The module validates data before you submit. It checks:

- Are all primary need codes valid DfE categories?
- Are dates logical? (No EHCPs finalised in the future. No children born after they started school.)
- Do numbers add up? (Total EHCPs should equal sum of all categories.)
- Are there anomalies? (Sudden increase or decrease from last year—is that real or an error?)

[WARMTH] Errors flagged. Explanations required for anomalies. You submit clean data, confident it's accurate.

**The Audit Trail**

[SERIOUS] Here's something local authorities increasingly care about. When they report numbers to the DfE, can they justify them? If central government queries a figure, can the LA trace it back to individual children?

[ENERGY] The module maintains a complete audit trail. Every number in your SEN2 return links to the underlying records. Child A contributes to this count. Child B contributes to that category.

[THOUGHTFUL] When questions arise—"Why did your EHCP numbers increase 15% this year?"—you can answer with evidence, not estimates.

**Historical Comparison**

[WARMTH] One of the most useful features: automatic comparison with previous years.

The module shows trends. Are your EHCP numbers growing? Shrinking? Stable? Are certain need categories changing? Are timelines improving or worsening?

[PAUSE]

[THOUGHTFUL] This isn't just about SEN2 compliance—it's about understanding your own context. If autism diagnoses are increasing locally, that shows up in your data. If you're meeting more timelines than last year, that's visible too.

[ENERGY] Data becomes insight. Numbers become stories about what's happening in your school and your community.

**The Timeline Reporting Challenge**

[CONCERN] One of the most sensitive aspects of SEN2 returns is timeline compliance. How many EHCPs were completed within the statutory 20-week timeframe?

[PAUSE]

[THOUGHTFUL] This is often where the most manual work happens. Calculating weeks between request and finalisation. Excluding school holidays. Accounting for exceptions. Checking whether each case met the deadline.

[WARMTH] The module handles all of this. Every EHCP in the system has its timeline automatically calculated. When SEN2 asks "How many met 20 weeks?", the answer is instant and accurate.

[SERIOUS] And if your numbers aren't good? The module doesn't hide that. But it does help you understand why. Which cases went over time? Where were the delays? What patterns emerge?

**One-Click Generation**

[ENERGY] Let's talk about the actual submission.

Different local authorities request data in different formats. Some want Excel files. Some have online portals. Some use legacy systems that require CSV in very specific structures.

[WARMTH] The module generates outputs in multiple formats. Choose your authority's preferred format. Download. Submit.

[THOUGHTFUL] We've worked with LAs across England to understand their requirements. The goal is that your export matches exactly what they need—no reformatting, no translation, no copying and pasting between systems.

**Supporting Local Authority Aggregation**

[WARMTH] Remember, your school's data feeds into your local authority's overall return. They aggregate data from every maintained school, academy, special school, and alternative provision.

[PAUSE]

[ENERGY] The module helps this process. Standardised data formats. Clear documentation. Quality-assured numbers. When every school submits clean data in consistent formats, the LA's aggregation becomes straightforward.

[THOUGHTFUL] Some LAs have told us that schools using this system submit data they can trust immediately—no back-and-forth queries, no corrections, no deadline stress.

**Beyond Compliance: Strategic Insight**

[SERIOUS] Here's my challenge to you. Don't treat SEN2 returns as pure compliance. Treat them as an annual opportunity to understand your SEND population.

[PAUSE]

[WARMTH] What do the numbers tell you? Are certain needs increasing? Are you meeting timelines better or worse than last year? How does your school compare to national trends?

[ENERGY] The module presents this analysis alongside the statutory return. Context. Benchmarks. Trends. Questions to consider.

[THOUGHTFUL] A SENCO who understands their data is a SENCO who can plan strategically. Who can anticipate needs before they become crises. Who can argue for resources with evidence.
