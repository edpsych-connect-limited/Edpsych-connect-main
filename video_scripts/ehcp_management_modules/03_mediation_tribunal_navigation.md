## Script

[CONCERN] The letter arrives. SENDIST. Those seven letters that make even experienced SENCOs' hearts sink.

[PAUSE]

[THOUGHTFUL] I remember the first time I received tribunal papers. I'd been working in education for years. I genuinely believed I'd done everything right for this child. But there it was—the family disagreed, fundamentally, with decisions that had been made.

[SLOW] And I thought: how did we get here?

[WARMTH] Here's what I've learned since then. Tribunals and mediations aren't failures. They're part of a system designed to ensure every child gets what they need. Parents have a legal right to challenge decisions. That's not adversarial—that's accountability.

[ENERGY] But—and this is crucial—many disputes don't need to reach tribunal. Many can be resolved earlier. And the Mediation and Tribunal module is designed to help you catch problems before they escalate, prepare when they do, and learn from every case.

**The Dispute Timeline: Understanding Escalation**

[THOUGHTFUL] Let me paint you a picture of how disputes typically unfold.

Stage one: Informal concerns. A parent mentions at pickup that they're not sure the provision is working. They email with questions. They seem... worried.

Stage two: Formal disagreement. The parent writes to request changes. They cite legislation. They mention "legal advice." The tone shifts.

Stage three: Mediation offer. Before any tribunal appeal, parents must be offered mediation. Some take it. Some don't.

Stage four: SENDIST appeal. The full tribunal process. Bundles, witness statements, hearings.

[PAUSE]

[SERIOUS] Here's what I want you to understand. The module tracks cases at every stage. Not because we expect you to fight parents—never that. But because at each stage, there are actions that can de-escalate, resolve, or at minimum ensure you're prepared.

**Early Warning Signs**

[WARMTH] The system flags patterns that experienced SENCOs recognise but might miss when overwhelmed.

Multiple emails in a short period. Requests for all documentation under Freedom of Information. Parents attending meetings with advocates or solicitors. Explicit mentions of "disagreement" or "appeal" or "not acceptable."

[ENERGY] When you see these flags, the module doesn't panic you. It prompts a reflection: Have you had a recent face-to-face with this family? Is there something unaddressed? Would a meeting—not formal, just human—help right now?

[THOUGHTFUL] I've seen so many disputes that could have been avoided if someone had simply called the parent and said, "I sense something's wrong. Can we talk?"

**Mediation Management**

[SLOW] Mediation is a gift. Seriously. It's a structured opportunity to resolve disagreements without the adversarial nature of tribunal. An independent mediator. A facilitated conversation.

[WARMTH] But mediation only works if you arrive prepared. The module helps you prepare a clear summary of the case: what's been provided, what outcomes have been achieved, where you believe there's disagreement, and—critically—what flexibility might exist.

[PAUSE]

Because here's the thing. Going into mediation with "we're right, you're wrong" guarantees failure. Going in with "here's what we've tried, here's what we could consider, here's what constraints we face"—that's how agreements happen.

[ENERGY] The module includes mediation preparation templates. Prompts that make you think through not just your position, but the family's perspective. What do they actually want for their child? What are their fears? What might they accept that isn't what they originally asked for?

**Tribunal Preparation: The Bundle**

[SERIOUS] Let's talk about SENDIST tribunals properly, because if you reach this stage, preparation is everything.

The tribunal bundle. A chronological collection of every relevant document. Assessments. EHCP drafts and finals. Professional reports. Meeting notes. Correspondence. All indexed, all paginated, all cross-referenced.

[CONCERN] I've seen schools scramble to assemble bundles in days. Searching through old emails. Finding reports that were never properly filed. Realising that crucial conversations were never documented.

[PAUSE]

[ENERGY] The Mediation and Tribunal module changes this. Because it integrates with everything else—Annual Reviews, the Golden Thread, all your documentation—the bundle essentially assembles itself. Documents already organised. Timeline already clear. Cross-references already established.

[WARMTH] You still need to review it. Add narrative. Ensure nothing's missing. But you're starting from a foundation, not a blank page.

**Working Statements and Evidence**

[THOUGHTFUL] The tribunal will want to hear from professionals who've worked with the child. That might be you. That might be the class teacher, the educational psychologist, the speech therapist.

The module helps you prepare working statements. Not legal documents—those need legal review—but clear, chronological accounts of what you've observed, what you've provided, what you believe the child needs.

[SLOW] And it helps you identify evidence gaps. If the parent claims provision wasn't delivered, what records do you have? If they claim outcomes weren't met, what assessment data contradicts or supports that?

**The Hearing Itself**

[WARMTH] I can't prepare you for the emotional weight of a tribunal hearing in a video. It's stressful. Even when you've done everything right, having your professional judgement examined is hard.

[PAUSE]

But I can tell you this. The SENCOs who feel most confident in tribunals are the ones who can say: "Every decision I made is documented. Every provision is recorded. Every conversation is captured. I may not have got everything right, but I can show you exactly what I did and why."

[ENERGY] The module gives you that confidence. Not false confidence—real, evidence-based clarity about your practice.

**Learning from Every Case**

[THOUGHTFUL] Whether a mediation succeeds or fails, whether a tribunal goes your way or not, there's learning.

The module captures outcomes. What was agreed in mediation? What did the tribunal direct? What would you do differently?

[SLOW] Over time, this builds institutional knowledge. Common dispute themes. Provision types that frequently cause disagreement. Outcome formulations that tribunals have found unclear.

[ENERGY] This isn't about winning and losing. It's about getting better. Because ultimately, every lesson learned means better EHCPs for future children, fewer disputes, more trust between families and schools.

**Closing**

[WARMTH] Look, I genuinely hope you never need this module in earnest. I hope every family you work with feels heard, respected, and confident that you're doing your best for their child.

[PAUSE]

[SERIOUS] But hope isn't a strategy. Some disputes are unavoidable. Some parents will disagree no matter what. Some decisions are genuinely contested territory.

[ENERGY] When that happens, you want to be prepared. Not defensive—prepared. Confident in your documentation. Clear about your evidence. Ready to engage constructively.

[SLOW] The Mediation and Tribunal module won't win cases for you. But it will ensure you're never caught unprepared.

[WARMTH] Next, let's move to something more optimistic: Phase Transfers. Helping children navigate the biggest transitions of their educational journey.
