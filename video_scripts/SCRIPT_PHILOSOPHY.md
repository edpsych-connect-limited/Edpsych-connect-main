# EdPsych Connect World - Video Script Philosophy

## The Problem with Our Current Videos
- Robotic pacing that sounds like text-to-speech reading a manual
- No emotional connection or storytelling
- Technical jargon without human context
- Headings and structure elements read aloud awkwardly
- No analogies to bridge understanding gaps
- Missing the warmth that makes learning memorable

## The EdPsych Connect Video Standard

### 1. Every Video Tells a Story
Not: "This module covers data protection protocols..."
Instead: "Picture this: It's Monday morning, and Mrs. Thompson discovers her school's student data has been compromised..."

### 2. Analogies Are Our Superpower
Not: "BYOD architecture means you retain database sovereignty..."
Instead: "Think of it like a safety deposit box at a bank. We're the bank - we provide the vault, the security, the systems. But you hold the only key. We literally cannot open your box without you."

### 3. Conversational, Not Presentational
Not: "In this section, we will examine..."
Instead: "Here's what keeps me up at night as an educational psychologist..."

### 4. Universal Appeal
- Parents should feel reassured
- Students should feel understood
- Professionals should feel empowered
- Administrators should feel confident

### 5. Emotional Beats
Every 30-60 seconds, there should be:
- A moment of recognition ("You've felt this, haven't you?")
- A moment of relief ("There's a better way")
- A moment of empowerment ("And now you can do this")

### 6. No Reading Structure
NEVER read:
- "Point one..."
- "Section two covers..."
- "As mentioned above..."
- Headers or titles as if they're sentences

### 7. Pacing That Breathes
- Pause before important points (not after "Point one")
- Speed up during exciting possibilities
- Slow down for emotional moments
- Use silence strategically

## Script Templates

### Opening Hook (15 seconds)
"[Relatable scenario or provocative question that creates tension]"

### The Problem (30 seconds)
"[Empathize with the struggle. Use 'you' language. Share specific pain points they'll recognize]"

### The Bridge (15 seconds)
"[Transition: 'What if...' or 'Imagine...' or 'Here's what changed everything...']"

### The Solution (60 seconds)
"[Tell the story of the solution through the user's eyes. Use present tense. Include an analogy]"

### The Proof (20 seconds)
"[Concrete outcome or transformation. Make it emotional, not just statistical]"

### The Invitation (10 seconds)
"[Warm, confident call to action that feels like an invitation to join something meaningful]"

## Voice Direction Notes

### Tone
- Warm expert sharing knowledge over coffee, not lecturing
- Occasional humor that lands naturally
- Vulnerability where appropriate ("I used to struggle with this too")
- Passionate but not preachy

### Pacing Markers
- [PAUSE] = 1-2 second pause for emphasis
- [SLOW] = Reduce pace for emotional weight
- [ENERGY] = Increase enthusiasm
- [THOUGHTFUL] = Reflective, as if remembering

### Emotion Markers
- [WARMTH] = Smile in voice
- [CONCERN] = Genuine care
- [EXCITEMENT] = Authentic enthusiasm
- [SERIOUS] = Weight of importance
