MEDICATION: Stimulants (methylphenidate/Ritalin, lisdexamfetamine/Elvanse) and non-stimulants (atomoxetine/Strattera, guanfacine). HOW THEY WORK: Increase dopamine/norepinephrine in prefrontal cortex - improve attention, impulse control, executive function. NOT: Sedatives, mind control, personality change. EFFECTS: 70-80% show significant improvement with medication. TEACHER ROLE: Observe and report effects (attention, behavior, appetite, mood), maintain confidentiality, no medication administration (usually parent/nurse), recognize medication isn't "fix" - still need accommodations and strategies. Medication + behavior support + accommodations = best outcomes.
