# ADHD Medication

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 80 seconds
**Audience:** Teachers

---

## Script

Teachers don't prescribe, but you are the eyes on the ground.
Medication (like methylphenidate) is not a 'sedative'. It's a stimulant. It stimulates the brakes of the brain.

It helps the child filter out distractions.
You might notice it wearing off around lunchtime or the end of the day—the 'rebound' effect.

Your feedback to the paediatrician is vital. "He focuses better in the morning but is irritable by 2pm." That is clinical gold.
