# The Three Presentations

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 75 seconds
**Audience:** Teachers

---

## Script

We don't call it ADD anymore. It's all ADHD, but with three presentations.

1. Predominantly Hyperactive-Impulsive: The classic "motor driven" child.
2. Predominantly Inattentive: The daydreamer. Often missed, especially in girls. They aren't disrupting the class, but they aren't learning.
3. Combined Type: The most common.

Understanding the presentation helps us tailor the support. The inattentive child needs prompts and engagement. The hyperactive child needs movement and channelled energy.
