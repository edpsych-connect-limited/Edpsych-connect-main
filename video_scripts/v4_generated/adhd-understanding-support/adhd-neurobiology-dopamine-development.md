# ADHD Neurobiology: Dopamine & Development

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 42 seconds

---

## Script

ADHD is not a behaviour problem. It is a developmental impairment of the brain's self-management system.
 
 I'm Dr. Scott. Let's look at the neuroscience.
 
 The ADHD brain has a dysregulated dopamine system. Dopamine is the neurotransmitter that allows us to regulate attention and motivation.
 In a neurotypical brain, dopamine bridges the gap between I should do this and I am doing this.
 In an ADHD brain, that bridge is often down.
 
 It is also a delay in the maturation of the prefrontal cortex—about 30% behind peers.
 So your 10-year-old student might have the impulse control of a 7-year-old.
 
 This is biology, not bad parenting.
