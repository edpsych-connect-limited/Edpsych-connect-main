# Instructional Accommodations

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 65 seconds
**Audience:** Teachers

---

## Script

Chunking is your best friend.
Don't give three instructions at once. The working memory bucket has a hole in it.

Give one instruction. Have them repeat it back.
Write it on the board.

Use 'dual coding'—say it and show it.
And break big tasks into micro-steps. "Write the date" is step one. Not "Write your essay".
