# Special Interests: Passion, Not Obsession

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 70 seconds
**Audience:** Teachers

---

## Script

We used to call them 'obsessions'. Now we call them 'Special Interests' or 'SpIns'.

These are the fuel of the autistic brain. They provide joy, regulation, and flow.

Don't take them away as a punishment. Use them as a bridge.
If they love trains, use trains to teach maths. Use trains to teach history.

When an autistic child is engaged in their special interest, their attention span is limitless. We need to harness that hyper-focus, not fight against it.
