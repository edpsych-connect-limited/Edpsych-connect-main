# Executive Function Difficulties

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 75 seconds
**Audience:** Teachers

---

## Script

Executive functions are the CEO of the brain. Planning, organising, initiating, shifting attention.

In autism, the CEO is often out to lunch.

A child might sit staring at a blank page not because they don't know the answer, but because they cannot initiate the motor sequence to pick up the pen. This is 'autistic inertia'.

It looks like laziness. It isn't. It's a disconnect between intention and action.
