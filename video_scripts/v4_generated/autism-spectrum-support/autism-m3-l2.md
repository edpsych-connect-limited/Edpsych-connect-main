# Creating Sensory-Friendly Environments

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 75 seconds
**Audience:** Teachers

---

## Script

You don't need a multi-million pound sensory room to make a difference.

Look at your classroom. Is it a visual riot of displays? That's cognitive noise.
Can you create a 'low arousal' corner? A pop-up tent or a quiet desk with headphones?

Sensory breaks should be proactive, not reactive. Don't wait for the meltdown.

Schedule 'heavy work'—carrying books, wall push-ups—to provide proprioceptive input. This calms the nervous system and gets the brain ready to learn.
