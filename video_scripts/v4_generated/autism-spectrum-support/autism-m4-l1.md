# Visual Supports: Making the Invisible Visible

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 60 seconds
**Audience:** Teachers, TAs

---

## Script

Spoken language is transient. Once I've said it, it's gone.
Visuals are permanent. They stay there to be processed.

For an autistic brain, auditory processing can be slow. By the time they've processed the first instruction, you're on the third.

Visual timetables, now-and-next boards, task checklists—these aren't just 'nice to haves'. They are the scaffolding of independence.

They reduce anxiety because they answer the four key questions: What am I doing? How long for? Who with? What happens next?
