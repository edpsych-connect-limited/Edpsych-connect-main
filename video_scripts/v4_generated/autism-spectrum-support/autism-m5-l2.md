# Meltdown Support

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 80 seconds
**Audience:** All Staff

---

## Script

A meltdown is not a tantrum. A tantrum is a behaviour choice to get something. A meltdown is a neurological event. It is a loss of control.

During a meltdown, the thinking brain is offline. You cannot reason with a child in this state.

Your goal is safety and de-escalation.
Low arousal. Reduce language. Back off. Give space.

Do not demand eye contact. Do not ask "Why did you do that?".

Afterwards, there will be a 'hangover' phase of exhaustion and often shame. This is not the time for a lecture. It's the time for repair and reassurance.
