# What is Autism? Neurodiversity Paradigm

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**Audience:** Teachers, SENCOs

---

## Script

Let's start by reframing how we see Autism. For decades, the medical model told us it was a 'disorder'—a list of deficits to be fixed.

I'm Dr. Scott, and I want you to look through a different lens: The Neurodiversity Paradigm.

Autism is not a broken operating system. It is a different operating system. It's a natural variation in human neurology, just like left-handedness.

The autistic brain is hyper-connected in some areas and hypo-connected in others. This creates a unique profile of spiky skills. You might see incredible attention to detail or pattern recognition, alongside significant challenges with sensory processing or social intuition.

Our job isn't to make autistic children 'normal'. It's to create an environment where their specific operating system can thrive. We stop trying to install Windows software on a Mac.
