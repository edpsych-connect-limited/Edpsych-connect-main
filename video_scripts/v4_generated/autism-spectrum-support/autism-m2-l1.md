# Social Communication Differences

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 80 seconds
**Audience:** Teachers

---

## Script

We used to talk about 'social deficits'. Now, we talk about the 'Double Empathy Problem'.

Research shows that autistic people communicate perfectly well with other autistic people. The breakdown happens between neurotypes.

For an autistic child, social rules can feel like a secret code that everyone else was born knowing. Eye contact, small talk, implied meanings—these are often manual processes, not automatic ones.

When we force eye contact, we are actually increasing their cognitive load. We are making it harder for them to listen.

Our goal is authentic communication, not compliance. We should value their directness and honesty, rather than trying to train them to be socially polite at the cost of their mental health.
