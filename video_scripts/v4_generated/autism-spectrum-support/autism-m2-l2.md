# Friendship and Peer Relationships

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 70 seconds
**Audience:** Teachers, Support Staff

---

## Script

Autistic children often play differently. They might prefer parallel play, or connecting through a shared interest rather than emotional sharing.

This doesn't mean they don't want friends. Many are desperately lonely.

But the playground is a chaotic, unwritten social minefield.

We can support them by creating structured social opportunities. Lego clubs, coding clubs—spaces where the activity mediates the interaction.

We focus on 'side-by-side' interaction rather than 'face-to-face'. It lowers the social pressure and allows genuine connections to form based on shared passions.
