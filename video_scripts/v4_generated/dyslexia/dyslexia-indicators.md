# Identifying Dyslexia: Indicators Across Ages

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 75 seconds
**Audience:** Teachers, Parents

---

## Script

Dyslexia looks different at age 5 than it does at age 15. As educators, we need to know the red flags at every stage.

In Early Years, don't look for reading. Look for speaking. Does the child struggle with nursery rhymes? Can they clap out syllables? Do they mix up sounds, saying 'par-cark' instead of 'car-park'? These are the earliest warning signs.

In Primary School, the gap widens. You'll see the classic reading struggle—slow, laborious decoding. But look closer. Look at their spelling. Is it inconsistent? Do they spell 'said' three different ways on the same page?

And look at their behaviour. Are they the class clown? Or the invisible child? Avoidance is the biggest symptom of all.

By Secondary School, the reading might be okay-ish. But the processing speed can't keep up. They can't take notes and listen at the same time. Their written work is brief and disorganised, despite them being articulate in discussion.

Our platform's 'Screener' looks for these age-specific patterns. It doesn't just ask "can they read?". It asks "how do they learn?".

Early identification changes lives. Let's not wait for them to fail.
