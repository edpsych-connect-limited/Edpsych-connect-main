# What is Structured Literacy?

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 65 seconds

---

## Script

If a child has dyslexia, 'wait and see' is a disaster. And 'more reading' isn't the answer. They need a specific type of teaching called Structured Literacy.
 
 You might know it as Orton-Gillingham.
 
 It has three non-negotiable rules.
 
 One: It must be Explicit. We don't hope they 'pick up' the rules of spelling. We teach them. We teach that 'ch' makes a /ch/ sound, and we teach *why*.
 
 Two: It must be Systematic. We follow a logical sequence. We don't teach 'ph' before we've mastered 'p' and 'h'. We build the house from the foundation up.
 
 Three: It must be Multisensory. We use eyes, ears, and hands. We trace letters in sand. We tap out sounds on our fingers. We lock the learning in through multiple pathways.
 
 Our 'Intervention Library' is packed with Structured Literacy resources. It gives you the lesson plans, the decodable texts, and the multisensory activities.
 
 It takes the guesswork out of intervention. It's not magic. It's evidence.
