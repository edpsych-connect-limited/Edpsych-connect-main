# Classroom Accommodations

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 26 seconds

---

## Script

Fairness is not giving everyone the same thing. It's giving everyone what they need.
 
 For a dyslexic student, accommodations are essential.
 Don't ask them to read aloud in class unless they've practised.
 Don't ask them to copy from the board—it's a torture test for working memory. Give them a handout.
 Give extra time.
 
 These aren't cheats. They are ramps for a wheelchair user.
