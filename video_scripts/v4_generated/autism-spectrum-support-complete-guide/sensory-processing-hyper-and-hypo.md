# Sensory Processing: Hyper and Hypo

**Speaker:** Dr. Scott Ighavongbe-Patrick
**Duration:** 38 seconds

---

## Script

Sensory processing is the foundation of regulation. You cannot learn if your body feels unsafe.
 
 We see two main profiles: Hyper-sensitive (the avoider) and Hypo-sensitive (the seeker).
 
 The avoider covers their ears at the fire drill. The seeker crashes into walls or chews their sleeve.
 
 Often, a child is both. They might be hyper-sensitive to sound but hypo-sensitive to proprioception—where their body is in space.
 
 If a child is rocking or humming, that is a regulatory strategy. It's functional. If we stop the behaviour without addressing the sensory need, we just increase the anxiety.
